Title 7. Offenses Against Property. Chapter 34 TX PENAL ยง 34.02. Money Laundering

What is Money Laundering?

Money laundering is the process of concealing the origin of money obtained from illegal sources such as drug trafficking, corruption, embezzlement, and gambling by saying that it came from a legitimate source.

What are the punishments?

Money laundering has many different punishments and penalties based on the amount of money that was laundered.

  • If $1,500 to $20,000 was laundered, then the penalty is 180 days to 2 years in jail with fines up to $10,000.
  • If $20,000 to $100,000 was laundered, then that makes it a third-degree felony resulting in a penalty of 2 to 10 years in jail with a $10,000 fine.
  • If $100,000 to $200,000 is laundered, then that makes it a second-degree felony resulting in a penalty of 2 to 20 years in jail with a $10,000 fine.
  • If over $200,000 is laundered, then that makes it a first-degree felony resulting in a penalty of 5 to 99 years in jail with a $10,000 fine; this is the worst penalty you can get for Money Laundering.
